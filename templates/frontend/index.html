    Top places to get the best bingsu

    After You

    Known for its heart attack-inducing Shibuya honey toasts, After You is second to none when it comes to the Japanese-style dessert. One other thing the venue makes very well — apart from the chocolate lava cake, sticky toffee pudding, brownie, and crepe cake — is the Kakigori. Though this shaved ice dessert originated in Japan, the look is actually a lot like bingsu. Stop at one of their branches which are scattered throughout Bangkok for their Milo Volcano Kakigori (THB 245) if you’re a fan of the cocoa-basted drink, or go for the Thai Tea Kakigori (THB 255) if you prefer to grab a taste more toward Thai flavours.

    Seobinggo

    This pioneering Korean dessert cafe started off right in the heart of Siam Square earlier this year, introducing Bangkok not only to the fluffy snow-like frozen milk treats but also to Korean-style toast, glued together by melted tteok (rice cake). Apart from injeolmi (soy powder) and red beans (both starting at B140), other toppings come with a scoop of ice cream including the best-seller mango cheese (B235 for small) and the popular new addition, melon popsicle (B350).

    Last updated one month ago

    Sulbing

    The mother of all bingsu brands, with more than 490 stores back home, Sulbing had humble beginnings 453 kilometers southwest of Seoul, in the port city of Busan. You may not rely on the name for the mellowest treats but do expect fun tweaks like melon (with tteok, red beans and corn flakes, B385) and yogurt melon (yogurt, cheesecake and half-melon, B395) on top of classic injeolmi and red bean (both B210). Alternatively, skip the ice and tuck into some toast or savory cheese stuffed tteok (both B140).

    Last updated on this week

    Ok Bing Sul

    This Bingsu franchise from Korea that takes pride in having one of the most delicate textures. There's 12 different Bingsu you can choose from. The top picks from us are the Strawberry Bingsu that uses fresh imported strawberries and the Brownie Bingsu that features a generous portion of homemade brownies. The best part of this place is the affordable price that will surely not put a hole in your wallet.

    Last updated on this month

    Han Bingo Korean Dessert Cafe

    This dessert shop with white furniture and wooden decor that gives off a very cozy vibe. There's even an outdoor section at the Emquartier branch for you to enjoy the view. The signature item here is the Bibimbap Bingo that draws inspiration from the all-time favorite Korean bibimbap dish. Colorful fruits like Mango and Kiwi cover the whole bowl, and an egg-shaped jelly just tops everything off perfectly. The taste? Pure delight!

    Last updated on Monday

    Gangnam Bingsu

    A very cute Korean dessert café run by a very kind Korean manager himself. The staff here are super friendly and they offer such a wide variety of Bingsu from juicy fresh Mango to savory cheese cubes. Other than the awesome Bingsu, there's also the Berry Cherry Brick Toast that is simply crispy golden toast topped off with fresh fruits and sweets. They also have a free blanket at your service in case you get cold!
